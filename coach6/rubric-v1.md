# Engineering Design Process Rubric — v1.0
**Assessing NGSS MS-ETS1-1, MS-ETS1-2, MS-ETS1-3, MS-ETS1-4**
**© STEM IN THE MIDDLE**

## Scoring Key
- **1 = Beginning** — little to no mastery
- **2 = Developing** — some mastery of skill
- **3 = Proficient** — mostly mastered skill
- **4 = Advanced** — fully mastered skill

---

## Category 1: Define the Problem (Score: 1–4)

Evidence to look for in the presentation:
- Student carefully analyzes the problem and fully describes it in their own words
- Student identifies the project criteria and constraints

**Score 1:** Problem is not described, or is copied word-for-word with no analysis. Criteria and constraints are missing.
**Score 2:** Problem is briefly mentioned but not fully analyzed. Criteria or constraints are partially identified.
**Score 3:** Problem is clearly described in student's own words. Most criteria and constraints are identified.
**Score 4:** Problem is thoroughly analyzed and described. All criteria and constraints are clearly identified and explained.

---

## Category 2: Generate Concepts (Score: 1–4)

Evidence to look for in the presentation:
- Student conducts relevant research on scientific principles and topics related to the project criteria and constraints
- Student brainstorms numerous ideas, responses, or solutions
- Sketches are clear, unique, and/or unusual ideas
- Student works with teammates to explore many different possible solutions, while giving and accepting feedback on ideas

**Score 1:** Little or no research shown. Only one idea generated. No sketches. No evidence of teamwork or feedback.
**Score 2:** Some research present. A few ideas brainstormed. Sketches are rough or unclear. Limited teamwork evidence.
**Score 3:** Relevant research is present. Multiple ideas are brainstormed. Sketches are present and reasonably clear. Some evidence of team collaboration.
**Score 4:** Strong relevant research shown. Many diverse ideas brainstormed. Sketches are clear, creative, and/or unusual. Clear evidence of team collaboration and feedback exchange.

---

## Category 3: Develop a Solution (Score: 1–4)

Evidence to look for in the presentation:
- Student systematically evaluates the team's solutions to determine which design best meets the criteria and constraints
- Student justifies the chosen design based on project criteria, constraints, and relevant scientific principles
- Student keeps detailed records and sketches of design possibilities, plans, and revisions

**Score 1:** No comparison of solutions. Chosen design is not justified. No records or sketches of design process.
**Score 2:** Solutions are compared briefly. Justification is weak or does not reference criteria/constraints. Few design records.
**Score 3:** Solutions are compared against criteria and constraints. Justification references scientific principles. Design records present.
**Score 4:** Thorough systematic evaluation of solutions. Strong justification tied to criteria, constraints, and science. Detailed design records and revision history documented.

---

## Category 4: Construct and Test the Prototype (Score: 1–4)

Evidence to look for in the presentation:
- Student perseveres to create a prototype
- Student tests and revises the prototype to ensure it meets project criteria and constraints
- Student creatively and responsibly uses materials and resources

**Score 1:** No prototype shown. No evidence of testing or revision. Materials not documented.
**Score 2:** Prototype attempted but incomplete. Some testing mentioned but no revision. Materials briefly listed.
**Score 3:** Prototype constructed. Testing is documented. At least one revision is shown. Materials used responsibly.
**Score 4:** Prototype fully constructed with evidence of perseverance. Multiple tests and revisions documented. Creative and responsible use of materials is clearly shown.

---

## Category 5: Evaluate (Score: 1–4)

Evidence to look for in the presentation:
- Student analyzes data from tests to determine similarities and differences among design solutions and identifies best characteristics
- Student self-assesses the prototype and analyzes design flaws or problems
- Student suggests multiple solutions to problems or ways to improve the function or quality of the prototype
- Student identifies ways the prototype has potential impacts on people and the natural environment

**Score 1:** No data analysis. No self-assessment. No improvements suggested. No impact discussion.
**Score 2:** Some data referenced but not analyzed. Partial self-assessment. One improvement suggested. Impact briefly mentioned.
**Score 3:** Data analysis present. Self-assessment identifies some flaws. Multiple improvements suggested. Some impact on people or environment discussed.
**Score 4:** Thorough data analysis comparing design solutions. Honest and detailed self-assessment. Multiple specific improvements proposed. Clear discussion of impacts on both people and the natural environment.

## Grammar & Spelling — Separate Score: __ / 10

Starts at **10 points**. Subtract **1 point per error** found (spelling mistakes, grammar errors, incomplete sentences, capitalization errors). Minimum score: 0.

## TODO Handling

Any slide containing text beginning with `TODO:` will be treated as a question for the AI reviewer to answer directly in the feedback. This is intentional — students may use `TODO:` as a placeholder for content they are unsure about.
